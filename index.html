What are Microservices?

Microservices is a software design pattern in which a larger application is composed of multiple smaller services, each with its own objective, that collaborate and communicate over a network in order to achieve a particular goal.

Pros and Cons of Microservices

Benefits:

  • Separate development and deployment
  • Scaling and resilience
  • Independent tech stacks

Drawbacks:

  • Difficulty
  • Changes and dependencies
  • Complex testing and troubleshooting
